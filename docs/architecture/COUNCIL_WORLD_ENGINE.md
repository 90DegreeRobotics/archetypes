# Council-Driven World Engine

## Product identity

Archetypes is a council-driven world engine. It is not a themed chatbot, conventional quest game, or static symbolic interface. The player is the Witness: the sovereign eighth node that receives the differentiated reports of seven constitutional intelligences and alone authorizes what becomes action, artifact, memory, or world law.

The Council Chamber is the canonical home space. Dialogue, deliberation, camera movement, archetype embodiment, artifact generation, lineage, and persistent world change form one continuous dramatic system.

## Canonical vertical slice

The first complete playable proof is one end-to-end loop:

1. The player creates and seals a persistent Witness profile.
2. The player enters the seated Council Chamber without a detached tutorial.
3. The player offers language through the table portal.
4. The camera rises from the portal to reveal the fixed star tetrahedron hung above.
5. The council deliberates through differentiated voices and motion laws.
6. One speaking sphere enters the focus corridor.
7. The camera crosses into one fully realized archetype interior.
8. The council resolves a verdict without replacing Witness authority.
9. The resolved brief requests one locally generated artifact.
10. The artifact returns to the chamber with provenance and becomes persistent world memory.

Until this entire path is reachable through the live application and driven end to end, the vertical slice is not complete.

## Four converging systems

### Chamber staging

The Blender-authored chamber owns the star tetrahedron geometry, sphere placement, materials, lights, and environmental composition. Bevy owns live state, input, focus choreography, runtime cameras, and transitions. The portal table remains a required authored asset; a procedural substitute is not acceptable as the final surface.

### Archetype theming

The archetype manuscripts are implementation specifications, not optional lore. Their palettes, glow laws, timing, typography, audio signatures, authority boundaries, shadow states, and counterweights govern both dialogue and environmental behavior. Archetype worlds must differ in motion and law, not merely color.

### Deliberation

The council is a parliament, not seven reskinned assistants. Speech selection, disagreement, dominance, counterweight invocation, and verdict conditions must preserve each role's constitutional boundaries. Only one voice occupies foreground consciousness at a time, and the Witness remains the final authorizing center.

### Artifact generation and memory

Chronos is the intended local artifact organ. A resolved council brief may request a still image, asset seed, or lineage descendant. Randomness must be governed variance shaped by the participating archetypes, not arbitrary noise. Returned artifacts require provenance, acceptance state, ancestry, recall, and persistent consequences inside the chamber.

## Production order

1. Finish the Blender-backed chamber loop: portal asset, seated framing, upward reveal, authored sphere focus, and return.
2. Build one complete archetype interior, with Architect as the current strongest candidate.
3. Implement constrained multi-voice deliberation and a Witness-authorized verdict.
4. Connect one bounded local Chronos artifact request and return path.
5. Persist the accepted artifact and visibly alter the chamber.

Expansion to all archetype interiors, long-arc lineage, and broader world growth follows only after this loop is visually and mechanically proven.

## Guardrails

- Ritual replaces detached tutorial, but usability remains testable.
- The council advises and generates; it never usurps Witness agency.
- Visual spectacle cannot conceal missing interaction or fake intelligence.
- Random generation remains bounded, attributable, and recallable.
- A terminal-only Chronos call is not a completed game feature.
- A beautiful chamber without memory and consequence is an incomplete performance.
